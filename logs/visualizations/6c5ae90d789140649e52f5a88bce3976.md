**Title:** The Last Rotation

**Genre:** Emotional drama / coastal mystery

**Setting:**  
A remote lighthouse on a black cliff above a rough northern sea, where the lantern room, the foghorn, and the tide-choked causeway are the only links between the keeper’s isolated home and the mainland.

**Story Premise:**  
When a lighthouse keeper discovers that the beam can briefly reveal things the sea has taken—lost boats, swallowed messages, even the final moments of the dead—he begins using it to search for one impossible thing: the truth about the disappearance of his only child during a storm years earlier. But the light has a rule: each time it is turned to the deep, it must take something from the keeper’s present life as payment. The more he uses it, the more the lighthouse erases the world he still has.

**Central Conflict:**  
To learn what happened to his child, the keeper must keep activating the beam against a forbidden maritime signal pattern that stabilizes the reveal. Each use permanently burns away a specific part of his own life from the present—first the scent of his home, then his voice on recordings, then the written evidence of his child’s existence in photographs and logs. The final use can show the exact fate of the child, but in exchange it will erase the lighthouse keeper’s legal identity from every record on the mainland, leaving him unable to prove he owns the lighthouse or even that he is who he says he is. If he chooses wrong, he loses the only home and history he has left.

**Complete Story Summary:**  
A solitary lighthouse keeper tends a weather-beaten tower above violent surf, maintaining a nightly ritual that keeps ships from the rocks below. During a storm-driven blackout, he notices an impossible reflection inside the lantern room: a fragment of a wrecked vessel drifting beneath the surface, lit from within as if the sea were a memory.
